WHAT’S IN THIS CATEGORY

Life-skills mini-games teach the practical numeracy and procedural sequences children meet outside the classroom: cleaning a space methodically, scrubbing a dish from edge to center, reading an analog clock, and counting coins to make purchases. Each game uses recognizable household objects and step-by-step task structure that mirrors how an adult would teach the activity in real life.

HOW IT HELPS LEARNING

Vacuuming uses a coverage-tracking grid: every square the cursor crosses changes color, so the child sees both progress and the missed areas. Wash Dishes models a circular scrubbing motion that builds wrist coordination. Read Clock cycles through hour and minute positions until both hands are read fluently. Money Math scaffolds from coin-recognition to two-step purchases (combine coins, count change).

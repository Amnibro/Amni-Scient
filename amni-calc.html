ENGINEERING MATH, WITHOUT THE INSTALL

Amni-Calc is a suite of mechanical engineering calculators that run entirely in your browser. No downloads, no license servers, no Python environment to configure. Open a tab, pick a calculator, enter loads and geometry, and get plotted results. The numerics run on WebAssembly for near-native speed; the interface is plain HTML and Plotly.

⚖

BEAM FEA

Cantilever, simply-supported, and fixed-end beam analysis. Shear, moment, and deflection diagrams from distributed or point loads. Stress and deflection checks against yield and allowable deflection ratios.

🛡

SEAL & GASKET DESIGN

Compression set, seating stress, and minimum gasket load for flanged joints. ASME-style m and y factor lookups, bolt-up preload calculations, and leak-rate estimation for elastomer and metallic seals.

BOLT TORQUE & PRELOAD

Friction-based torque for any bolt diameter, grade, and thread class. Nut-factor method (T = KDF) and detailed thread/face friction split. Yield checks, preload scatter bands, and stretch calculations for critical joints.

BELLEVILLE & COIL SPRINGS

Belleville washer load-deflection curves (Almen-Laszlo), stack configurations (series/parallel), stress at inner edge, and coil compression spring sizing with Wahl stress correction. Cross-section optimizer and spring catalog.

THERMAL ANALYSIS

Steady-state conduction through layered walls, convection and radiation boundary conditions, and transient temperature response for lumped bodies. Material database with k, cp, and density for common alloys and polymers.

WHY IN-BROWSER ENGINEERING TOOLS

Traditional engineering software falls into two camps. On one end are full FEA suites — ANSYS, Abaqus, SolidWorks Simulation — which cost thousands of dollars per seat and require Windows workstations with non-trivial setup. On the other end are hand-calc spreadsheets: cheap and portable but error-prone, unversioned, and full of magic numbers from handbooks that nobody quite remembers.

In-browser calculators split the difference. They run anywhere a modern browser does — desktops, laptops, Chromebooks, tablets. They can be deployed instantly to a team without IT approval for new software installs. And because the computation runs locally, proprietary design inputs never leave the device. The calculator page loads, executes your numerics against your inputs, and renders results — all without a round trip to a server.

WebAssembly matters here. JavaScript is great for UI but slow for dense numerical work like solving stiffness matrices or integrating beam deflection curves. Amni-Calc compiles the numerical kernels from systems languages to WASM, delivering 5–20x speedups over equivalent JavaScript. A beam analysis with 1000 elements renders in under 50 ms; a thermal transient with fine time steps in under 100 ms. Fast enough that users treat the calculator as interactive, iterating through design variations the way they would a spreadsheet.

Interactive charts are the other half. An engineer comparing two gasket materials or three spring stacks benefits from seeing both curves on a single plot with a hover-readout. Plotly handles this well in the browser, and because everything is client-side, zooming and panning feel instantaneous — no server re-render, no loading spinner between clicks.

TYPICAL WORKFLOW

1. PICK A CALCULATOR

Sidebar lists every module. Each opens as a single tab with inputs on the left, charts and tables on the right. No project files, no save dialogs — state lives in the URL and in browser storage.

2. ENTER GEOMETRY & LOADS

Numeric inputs use sensible engineering defaults. Unit toggles for SI and US customary. Material pickers pull from the shared database. Invalid combinations flag inline rather than silently producing garbage.

3. READ THE CHARTS

Interactive Plotly plots show the answer: shear/moment diagrams, load-deflection curves, temperature profiles, stress circles. Hover for exact values. Compare configurations by stacking them on the same plot.

4. EXPORT OR ITERATE

Right-click any chart to save as PNG for a report. Change inputs and the plot updates live. All computation is local, so large parameter sweeps run without network latency.

PRIVACY & DATA

Amni-Calc runs entirely in your browser. Design inputs, material choices, and calculated results stay on your device — they are never transmitted to Amni-Scient or any third party. Browser local storage is used only to remember UI preferences (theme, last-opened tab) and cached material data. No accounts, no telemetry, no analytics of your engineering work.

The app is ad-supported on the informational landing page (this page). The calculator app itself, at /calc/, carries no ads so you can focus on the work.

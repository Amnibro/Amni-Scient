MATERIALS HANDBOOK

Use the searchable database for exact entries, then use these handbook notes to screen families fast. Values below are typical room-temperature ranges for early design only; final design should always be checked against the exact alloy, temper, heat treatment, mill cert, and governing code.

Carbon & Alloy Steel

Best first stop when stiffness, cost, machinability, and weldability matter more than mass. Great for shafts, frames, weldments, brackets, and bolted structure. Watch corrosion, coatings, weld HAZ hardness, and low-temperature toughness.

High ELow CostNeeds Corrosion Plan

Stainless & Corrosion Alloys

Choose when washdown, humidity, chloride splash, food contact, or appearance matter. Austenitics are forgiving but not high-strength; precipitation-hardened grades raise strength but need heat-treatment control and crack-sensitivity review.

CorrosionLower kGalling Risk

Aluminum & Light Alloys

Good for weight reduction, thermal conductivity, and rapid machining. Excellent for enclosures, heat sinks, lightweight structures, and transport hardware. Check fatigue detail quality, thread bearing, creep near heat, and galvanic pairing with steels.

Low DensityHigh kThermal Growth

Bronze & Copper Alloys

Strong choice for bushings, wear plates, electrical hardware, and marine/wet-service parts. Sliding wear behavior can be excellent, but density is high and yield is usually modest versus steels. Confirm lead content and galvanic compatibility.

Bearing UseGood ConductivityHeavy

Engineering Polymers

Use for low-friction guides, chemical resistance, electrical isolation, light load structures, and low-noise mechanisms. The real traps are creep, moisture pickup, thermal expansion, and property loss at heat. Check continuous-use temperature, not just melting point.

Low MassCreep ReviewChemical Fit

Elastomers

Seal success is mostly about fluid compatibility, compression set, temperature, and extrusion resistance. Hardness alone is not enough. Screen NBR for general oils, EPDM for water/steam, and FKM for fuels and hot oil; verify swell and low-temp behavior.

SELECTION CHECKLIST

Load Path

Screen yield, buckling, bearing, thread strip, local contact stress, and fatigue before comparing cost. Strength-only comparisons miss stiffness and stability failures.

Temperature

Use continuous-use temperature, creep limits, temper loss, and seal compression-set data. Hot excursions often control before nominal room-temperature strength does.

Environment

Check chloride, caustic, steam, fuels, UV, vacuum, and ozone separately. The wrong environment assumption can invalidate an otherwise correct material pick.

Contact Pair

Look at galling, galvanic difference, hardness mismatch, seal swell, and thermal expansion mismatch. Assemblies fail at interfaces, not just in single materials.

Manufacturing

Confirm weldability, castability, machinability, heat-treatment distortion, coating route, and supply chain form factor before carrying a material forward.

Compliance

Tie every short list back to ASTM, ASME, SAE, AMS, FDA, NSF, UL, or NACE requirements early so the project does not need a late material restart.

Theory, Formulas & Standards Referenced

Amni-Calc implements classical closed-form engineering methods and selected code-based checks for preliminary sizing and conceptual analysis. Results are always first-principles approximations — final designs must be validated against current editions of the governing codes and by a licensed professional engineer.

Beam Bending

Euler-Bernoulli theory: EI y″″ = w(x). Superposition of point, distributed, and moment loads via influence functions. Deflection at load δ = PL³/48EI for simply supported mid-span.

Refs: Shigley, Roark's Formulas for Stress & Strain

Combined Stress & Mohr's Circle

Principal stresses from 2D state: σ1,2 = (σx+σy)/2 ± √[((σx-σy)/2)² + τxy²]. Von Mises equivalent stress gates yield.

Refs: Shigley Ch. 3-4; ASME VIII Div 2 Sec 5

Section Properties

Area, centroid, second moment of area I, section modulus S = I/c, radius of gyration r = √(I/A) for I-beams, HSS, channels, custom shapes via parallel-axis theorem.

Refs: AISC Steel Construction Manual

Bolt Preload & Torque

T = K · d · Fi with nut factor K = 0.15-0.20. Recommended preload for static Fi = 0.75 Fp, reusable joints 0.75·Sp·At.

Refs: Shigley Ch. 8; VDI 2230; ISO 898-1

Helical Springs

Shear stress τ = Kw · 8FD/(πd³), rate k = Gd⁴/(8D³Na). Bergsträsser/Wahl factor corrects curvature. Belleville disc via Almen-Laszlo.

Refs: SAE HS-795; ASTM A228

O-Ring & Elastomer Seals

Squeeze 15-30% typical for static radial; 10-20% face. Compression-set and swell bound service envelope. Internal node FEA approximates contact region and peak stress.

Refs: Parker O-Ring Handbook ORD-5700; ISO 3601

Column Buckling

Transition slenderness Cc = √(2π²E/Sy). Euler for long columns: σcr = π²E/(KL/r)². Johnson parabolic for intermediate: σcr = Sy[1 - Sy(KL/r)²/(4π²E)].

Refs: AISC 360; Shigley Ch. 4

Shaft Torsion & Critical Speed

Polar moment J = π(Do⁴-Di⁴)/32; max shear τ = Tr/J; twist θ = TL/GJ. Dunkerley ωn = √(g/δ) for first whirl.

Refs: ASME B106.1M; Den Hartog

Fillet Welds (AWS)

Throat t = 0.707 a for equal-leg fillet. Allowable shear on throat = 0.30 FEXX. Weld-group polar moment Jg = Ix + Iy for eccentrically loaded groups.

Refs: AWS D1.1; AISC 360 Ch. J2

Bearing Life

Basic rating life L10 = a1 (C/P)p, with p = 3 for ball, 10/3 for roller bearings. Reliability factor a1 for L5, L1 adjustments.

Refs: ISO 281; ABMA 9/11

Fatigue (High-Cycle)

Four failure criteria on the Goodman diagram: Goodman, Soderberg, Gerber (parabolic), ASME-Elliptic. Marin factors modify endurance limit Se = kakbkckdkekf Se'.

Refs: Shigley Ch. 6; ASTM E466

Vibration & Isolation

Natural frequency fn = (1/2π)√(k/m). Transmissibility TR = √(1+(2ζr)²)/√((1-r²)²+(2ζr)²) where r = f/fn.

Refs: Rao, Mechanical Vibrations; ISO 10816

Gears (Lewis Bending)

Tooth bending stress σ = WtPd/(FY). Geometry: module m, pressure angle 20°/25°, addendum a = m, dedendum b = 1.25m.

Refs: AGMA 2001-D04; Shigley Ch. 13-14

Fluid Flow (Moody)

Darcy-Weisbach Δp = f (L/D)(ρV²/2). Friction factor f from Moody chart — Colebrook-White implicit or Swamee-Jain explicit. Reynolds Re = ρVD/μ.

Refs: Crane TP-410; Moody (1944)

Pumps & NPSH

NPSHa = (Patm-Pvap)/(ρg) - Hs - HL. Affinity laws: Q∝N, H∝N², P∝N³. Specific speed Ns = N√Q/H3/4.

Refs: Hydraulic Institute Standards; Karassik

Heat Transfer

Conduction q = -kA(dT/dx), convection q = hAΔT, radiation q = εσA(Ts⁴-T∞⁴). Fin efficiency η = tanh(mL)/(mL).

Refs: Incropera; Mills

Heat Exchangers

LMTD ΔTlm = (ΔT1-ΔT2)/ln(ΔT1/ΔT2). NTU-effectiveness for unknown outlet temps. Correction factor F for shell-and-tube.

Refs: TEMA; Kern; Kakac

Pressure Vessels

Thin-wall circumferential σh = PD/2t, longitudinal σl = PD/4t. Thick-wall Lamé stress distribution. Code-based minimum thickness per ASME VIII-1 UG-27.

Refs: ASME BPVC VIII-1; EN 13445

Thermodynamic Cycles

Carnot η = 1 - TL/TH. Otto η = 1 - 1/rk-1. Diesel, Brayton, Rankine efficiencies. Refrigeration COPR = QL/Wnet.

Refs: Cengel & Boles; Moran

HVAC & Psychrometrics

Humidity ratio W = 0.622 Pw/(P-Pw), enthalpy h = 1.006T + W(2501+1.86T), dew point from water-vapor saturation.

Refs: ASHRAE Fundamentals Ch. 1

Electrical & Power

Ohm V = IR, AC active P = VI cosφ, reactive Q = VI sinφ. 3-phase line-to-line P = √3 VLL I cosφ. RC/RL time constants τ = RC, τ = L/R.

Refs: IEEE Std 141 (Red Book)

Motors (3-Phase)

Synchronous speed Ns = 120f/P, slip s = (Ns-N)/Ns. Shaft torque T = 9549 PkW/Nrpm. FLA I = P/(√3 V · pf · η).

Refs: NEMA MG-1; IEEE 112

NEC Wire Sizing

75°C Cu ampacity per NEC Table 310.16. 125% continuous-load multiplier, ambient temperature and conductor-count derating per 310.15. Voltage drop Vd = 2I · RΩ/ft · L.

Refs: NFPA 70 (NEC) 310.15/310.16

Electrochemistry

Nernst E = E° - (RT/nF) ln Q. Tafel η = a + b log|i|. Butler-Volmer for electrode kinetics. Faraday m = ItM/(nF) for deposition.

Refs: Bard & Faulkner; Newman

Battery Sizing

Peukert runtime t = C/Ik where k ≈ 1.1-1.3 for Li-ion. Pack voltage = Vcell · S-count, capacity = Ccell · P-count, with thermal derating.

Refs: IEC 62620; UL 1973

Combustion

Stoichiometric AFR for CxHy: AFRs = (x+y/4) · 4.76 · 28.97 /(12x+y). Lambda λ = AFR/AFRs. Adiabatic flame Tad = Ti + LHV/((AFR+1)cp).

Refs: Turns, Combustion; Glassman

Unit Conversion & Constants

SI / Imperial conversion for length, force, pressure, energy, power, temperature, torque, viscosity, density. Engineering constants (g, R, σSB, NA) to 6 significant figures.

Refs: NIST SP 811; BIPM SI Brochure

Materials Screening & Allowables

Early screening should compare E/ρ, yield, conductivity, CTE, corrosion behavior, and continuous-use temperature together. Final design values must come from the exact alloy, temper, heat treatment, and certified product form, not family averages.

Refs: ASM Handbooks; MMPDS; ASTM / ASME material specs

Heat Treatment & Temper State

Alloy name without condition is incomplete engineering data. 4140 annealed and 4140 Q&T are different materials in practice, just like 17-4 H900 and H1150 or 7075-T6 and T73. Machining distortion, SCC resistance, hardness, and toughness all follow the condition.

Refs: ASM Heat Treating; AMS / ASTM temper and hardness specifications

Finishes, Coatings & Galvanic Control

Surface treatments solve different problems: zinc and paint for carbon steel corrosion, anodize for aluminum oxide control, nitriding and hard chrome for wear, and electroless nickel for coverage. Galvanic separation, sealing, and contact area ratio matter as much as the finish itself.

Refs: ASM Surface Engineering; MIL / ASTM coating specifications

Threads, Preload Scatter & Joint Stiffness

Bolt strength is rarely the first problem; clamp load scatter, surface embedding, and joint stiffness ratio are. Torque is only an indirect preload method through a noisy nut factor, which is why washer stack, lubrication, and engagement length deserve as much attention as proof strength.

Refs: VDI 2230; ISO 898-1; ASTM F606

Tribology & Lubrication

Rolling-bearing life, plain-bearing PV, lubricant film thickness, and surface finish are all parts of the same problem. Systems that look safe on contact stress still fail when contamination, starvation, or edge loading collapse the film and shift the mechanism into boundary wear.

Refs: ISO 281; Hamrock; SKF / NSK application handbooks

Compressible Flow & Pneumatics

Air systems stop behaving like incompressible utilities once Mach number, choked flow, and line volume matter. Cylinder force, valve Cv, receiver volume, and regulator droop need to be reviewed together or the machine will miss cycle time even if the static pressure looked adequate.

Refs: Crane TP-410; ASME fluids references; pneumatic component application guides

Fits, Tolerances & Surface Texture

Dimensioning is only half the interface. Clearance or interference fits, waviness, and roughness together decide bearing load distribution, seal life, and assembly force. Start from a hole-basis system, then verify thermal growth and coating stack-up.

Refs: ISO 286; ASME Y14.5; Machinery's Handbook

Gaskets, Flanges & Joint Integrity

Leak-tight joints depend on gasket stress, flange stiffness, relaxation, and surface finish. Static stress checks miss the actual failure mode when clamp load falls below seating stress after thermal cycle, creep, or embedding.

Refs: ASME PCC-1; ASME B16.5; EN 1591

Pipe, Flanges & Pressure Boundaries

Pipe schedule, flange class, gasket type, bolting, and assembly procedure are a single design system. Choosing any one of them in isolation is how teams end up with joints that meet stress checks but leak in startup, thermal shock, or maintenance reassembly.

Refs: ASME B31.3; ASME B16.5; ASME B16.20; ASME PCC-1

Manufacturing Allowances & Stack-Up

Coating build, corrosion allowance, weld shrink, machining stock, and thermal growth are real dimensions that belong in the design model. The fastest way to lose a good concept is to size everything at nominal and discover after release that finish, distortion, or service temperature consumed the fit.

Refs: Machinery's Handbook; ASM fabrication and finishing references

Corrosion & Environment

Environment screening should separate chlorides, caustics, steam, hydrocarbons, UV, and vacuum rather than bundling them into a single “chemical resistance” label. Crevices, deposits, and trapped condensate routinely dominate open-exposure assumptions.

Refs: NACE; ASM Corrosion; Parker compatibility guides

Privacy & Architecture

All calculations run locally in your browser. No inputs, intermediate values, or results leave your device. The optional engineering AI assistant connects either to your own local server or runs fully in-browser via WebLLM using cached quantized weights. Source is served as a single static bundle — no build-step, no framework lock-in.

Built and maintained by Anthony Reffelt, an electrochemical engineer focused on open scientific tooling. The calculation kernel is written in Rust and compiled to WebAssembly. If you find this useful, consider tipping on Ko-fi.
